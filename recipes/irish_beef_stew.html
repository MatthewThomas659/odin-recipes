Hearty Irish Beef Stew

Description

A comforting, slow-simmered classic, Irish beef stew is rich, warming, and deeply satisfying. Tender chunks of beef are gently braised with root vegetables and herbs, creating a thick, savoury broth perfect for cold evenings or a hearty Sunday meal.

Ingredients

  • 800g stewing diced beef
  • 2 tbsp plain flour
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 2 parsnips, sliced
  • 3 large potatoes, cubed
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp tomato puree
  • 500ml beef stock
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley

Steps

  1. Toss the beef in flour, seasoning with salt and pepper
  2. heat oil in a large pot over medium heat and brown the beef in batches. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, saute the onion and garlic until softened.
  4. Stir in tomato puree and cook for 1 minute.
  5. Return beef to the pot and add stock, thyme, and bay leaves.
  6. Bring to a simmer, cover, and cook for 1 hour.
  7. Add carrots, parsnips, and potatoes. Simmer for another 45-60 minutes until tender.
  8. Remove bay leaves, adjust seasoning, and garnish with parsley before serving
