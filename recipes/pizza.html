Pizza Recipe

The Ultimate Slice of Comfort

There's a reason pizza is a global obssession: it's the perfect canvas for culinary creativity. From the satisfying crunch of a stone-fired crust to the molten pull of premium mozzerella and the bright tang of vine-ripened tomato sauce, a great pizza is a masterclass in balanced textures and flavours. Whether you're a fan of the minimalist Neapolitan tradition or a toppings enthusiast, pizza is more than just a meal it's a universal language of comfort, shared one delicious slice at a time.

Ingredients

      Flatbread Dough: A rectangular, rustic-style base that providesnice foundation
      Large Pepperoni Slices: Thinly sliced
      Mozzarella Cheese
      Tomato Sauce
      Red and Yellow Peppers
      Grated Paremsan
      Dried Herbs & Red Pepper Flakes
      Cherry Tomatoes on the Vine

Steps

To recreate this rustic, pepperoni-topped flatbread, follow these simple steps to ensure a crispy base and perfectly melted toppings:

    • Prep the Dough and Oven
      Prehead your oven to 220 C. If you have a pizza stone, place it inside to get screaming hot.
      Stretch your dough into an oval shape on a piece of parchment paper or a floured wooden board. Aim for a rustic look rather than a perfect circle.
      Layer the Base
      Spread a thin, even layer of tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small boarder for the crust to puff up.
      Scatter a generous amount of shredded mozzarella or torn fresh buffalo mozzeralla acros the sauce.
      Add the Toppings
        Place the large pepperoni slices so they slightly overlap, ensuring every bite gets some spice.
        Sprinkle the finely diced red and yellow bell peppers into the gaps between the pepperoni.
        Season with a pinch of dried oregano and a dusting of grated Parmesan cheese for that extra savory kick.
      Bake and Server
    1. Slide the flatbread into the oven and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbling and the edged of the crust are golden brown.
    2. Garnish with a final sprinkle of fresh herbs and red pepper flakes if you like a little heat.
    3. Plate it alongside a cluster of fresh cherry tomatoes on the vine.
